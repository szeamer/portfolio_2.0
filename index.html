I'm

Security/HCI Researcher, Designer, Developer, Fine Artist

I'm a senior at Wellesley College graduating in June with a degree in Media Arts and Sciences.

I'm a strong designer, researcher, and programmer, as well as an excellent communicator with a knack for picking up new skills fast. I'm published in CHI 2020 and am working on a followup paper about locus of control and its impact on user security behaviors. I've applied my skills with data to digitizing Wellesley's 100+ years of course records. In my research job and in my free time, I've challenged myself to create beautiful, data-driven designs and illustrations.

EDUCATION

I am studying my BA in Media Arts and Sciences at Wellesley College, with a GPA of 3.6/4.0.

I have taken coursework spanning data science, security, design, and the humanities.

LANGUAGE

I speak Hungarian and English fluently and am conversational in French. I also have basic knowledge of Mandarin, German, and Russian.

TECHNICAL SKILLS

I am most comfortable working with Python, which I have used extensively for data analysis and other scripting tasks. I am also familiar with Java, HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I am proficient with standard office software as well as Figma and Adobe Creative Suit.

HOW I WORK

My greatest strengths are my facility with language and communication and my ability to engage with and solidify abstract ideas. I work best in a supportive, intellectual environment where individuals have a high degree of ownership of their work.

INTERESTS

In my free time, I design knitted garments, bake, and read - mostly historical nonfiction, lately. I also play Hearthstone, Slay the Spire, and Risk of Rain 2.
